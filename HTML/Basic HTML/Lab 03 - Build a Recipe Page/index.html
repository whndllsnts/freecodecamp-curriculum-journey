Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. This recipe uses pork as the main ingredient. Other proteins and seafood can also be used. Beef, shrimp, fish are commonly used to cook sinigang.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork ribs
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1–2 pieces radish, sliced
  • 1 bunch water spinach (kangkong)
  • 10–12 pcs string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1–2 pcs long green chili (optional)
  • Okra (optional)
  • 1 packet Sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 8 cups water
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 packet Sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)

Instructions

  1. First, place the pork, onion, and tomatoes into a pot and pour in 8 cups of water. Bring the pot to a boil and remove any scum that rises to the top.
  2. Next, lower the heat and let the pork simmer for 40–60 minutes until it becomes tender.
  3. After the pork softens, add the sliced radish and cook for about 3 minutes to let it soften slightly.
  4. Then, add the eggplant and cook for another 2 minutes, allowing it to absorb the broth.
  5. After that, add the string beans and let them cook for about 3 minutes.
  6. Once the vegetables are partly cooked, pour in the sinigang mix and season the soup with salt, pepper, and fish sauce according to your taste.
  7. Next, add the long green chili and okra and let them cook for 1–2 minutes to release flavor.
  8. Finally, add the kangkong leaves and turn off the heat, allowing the remaining heat of the soup to cook the greens gently.
